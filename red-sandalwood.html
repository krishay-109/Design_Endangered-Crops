RED SANDALWOOD

Why should we protect red sandalwood?

Red sandalwood is an endangered crop worth conservation for several reasons. First, it is ecologically significant and supports biodiversity. The ecosystem of red sandalwood also supports several ecosystems. The red sandalwood is also of immense economic value because of its highly prized wood. The use of the wood is majorly in traditional crafts, furniture, and medicinal applications and, therefore, gives economic benefits to communities with its sustainable harvesting. Its special features and cultural value in different areas demand its conservation. Its over-exploitation accompanied with habitat destruction is pushing it to decline, therefore requires conservation efforts to be sustained so that it may survive for future generations, and also to maintain the ecosystems' operation.

Endangerment Reasons

Red sandalwood (Pterocarpus santalinus) primarily faces over-exploitation as its highly prized timber is used in furniture and craftwork, in addition to indigenous medicine preparation. There is an increased poaching rate for the black market from the wild stock because of the illegal logging operation clearing more areas of this tree. Deforestation and land conversion destroy habitats, which decreases the places in which this tree naturally occurs, thus limiting its capacity to renew. It has a slow growth rate, making the species particularly vulnerable to overharvesting. Climate change is also likely to threaten it by changing growth patterns and distribution, while competition from invasive species may further deter it from survival. Thus, addressing these challenges with sustained management and legal protection is more key to conservation of red sandalwood.
