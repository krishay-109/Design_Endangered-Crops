MUSLI

Why should we protect Musli?

It is essential that the endangered crop Musli be protected. First, Musli is a plant held in high esteem in traditional and herbal medicine because of its medicinal values, with its many side benefits, which include acting as an aphrodisiac and improving the overall energy level of the user. Its medicinal properties thus add to biodiversity in herbal medicine, making it a crucial resource both for the pharmaceutical industries and the local communities. Other advantages also include taking part in the local economy, particularly in village regions. Their factory is frequently impacted by habitat destruction that might be caused by overexploitation and unsustainable farm practices, with the risk of its unavailability and the people's very survival who are dependent on it. Protect Musli is an effort to promote, above all, the practice of sustainable agriculture by preserving traditional knowledge and making this precious resource available to future generations.

Endangerment Reasons

Musli or the Chlorophytum borivilianum is threatened by a combination of interrelated factors: Over-exploitationthe species is mainly overutilized as people seek traditional medicinal properties, partly because demand normally exceeds sustainable quantities. Loss of habitat caused by the expansion of agriculture and urbanization together with deforestation has reduced its natural habitats where the species may be allowed to grow naturally. With regard to the intensive cultivation methods alongside the lack of awareness about the need for its conservation, the problem causes a poor regeneration of the already existing populations. Even climate change poses a threat due to its influence on the ecosystems where Musli grows, affecting survival chances and the ability to reproduce. These factors ultimately result in a fragile position for Musli, and there is immediate need to undertake some form of conservation to save this crop along with its ecological and economic values.
